How It Works

A Persistent Antimicrobial Barrier for Hard, High-Touch Surfaces

VitaCoat is described as a transparent bonded layer that combines natural citrus bioflavonoids with a durable SiO₂ matrix. The result is a surface treatment positioned to remain active between routine cleaning cycles rather than only at the moment of disinfection.

Request Technical ReviewOpen Documentation

1. Bonded Surface Layer

The coating is positioned as a thin transparent layer that adheres to hard, non-porous substrates.

2. Active Antimicrobial Chemistry

Natural citrus bioflavonoids are presented as the core active ingredient for antimicrobial performance.

3. SiO₂ Matrix Support

The silicon dioxide matrix supports adhesion, surface durability, and controlled residual action.

Conventional Spray-and-Wipe

Works mainly at the moment of application and does not create a persistent bonded layer for the period between routine cleaning events.

VitaCoat Residual Protection Model

Creates a bonded antimicrobial surface layer positioned to remain active across normal-use intervals between routine cleaning cycles.

VitaCoat is positioned as a supplementary hygienic surface treatment and should be used alongside established cleaning and disinfection protocols.
