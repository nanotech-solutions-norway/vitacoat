How It Works

Two Ingredients. One Durable Barrier.

VitaCoat fuses natural citrus bioflavonoids with a durable SiO₂ matrix to form a transparent antimicrobial coating engineered for hard, non-porous surfaces. The result is a science-driven surface barrier designed to remain active between cleaning cycles for up to six months under normal use conditions.

Core Technology

The Citrox–SiO₂ System

Citrox bioflavonoids

Citrox is a proprietary blend of citrus-derived polyphenolic bioflavonoids that provides the active antimicrobial mechanism at the surface interface. The material is positioned as naturally derived, effective at low concentrations, and well suited to high-touch environments where surface safety and regulatory scrutiny matter.

SiO₂ matrix

The silicon dioxide matrix acts as the structural backbone of the system. It forms a tightly bonded, optically transparent film on hard, non-porous substrates and distributes the bioflavonoid actives across the surface while protecting them through the coating’s service life.

Mechanism of Action

Three-Part Antimicrobial Mechanism

1. Chemical Kill on Contact

Bioflavonoids at the surface interface interact with microbial cell membranes and compromise membrane integrity on contact.

2. Anti-Adhesion Surface Character

The SiO₂ matrix modifies roughness and surface energy, making it more difficult for microorganisms to anchor and initiate biofilm formation.

3. Controlled Residual Action

The matrix acts as a reservoir that maintains active compound availability at the surface over time, supporting protection between cleaning events.

This layered mechanism is intended to address microbial threat at multiple stages of surface interaction — from first contact through to colonization prevention — rather than relying on a single point of action.
Surface Physics

Hydrophobicity and the Easier-Clean Effect

Reduced soil adhesion

Contaminants bond less aggressively to treated surfaces, reducing the scrubbing force and chemical load needed during cleaning.

Lower chemical burden

Easier release of soils means lower detergent and disinfectant demand per cycle in many routine-use contexts.

Extended coating life

Gentler cleaning conditions help preserve the SiO₂ matrix over time, supporting longer stable performance under normal use.

Technology Clarification

VitaCoat Is Not a Photocatalytic Coating

VitaCoat does not rely on titanium dioxide (TiO₂) photocatalysis and does not require UV activation to function. Its antimicrobial action is based on the Citrox bioflavonoid mechanism and the SiO₂ delivery matrix, not on light-dependent reactive oxygen chemistry.

This distinction is operationally important because many of the relevant high-touch environments — healthcare corridors, public transit interiors, offices, and hospitality touchpoints — are low-UV or fully indoor environments where photocatalytic alternatives can be severely attenuated.

Light-independent performance

  • No UV activation required
  • Designed for indoor and low-UV environments
  • Consistent mechanism in artificial light or darkness
  • Compatible with routine cleaning and facility operations
Operational Integration

Supporting Routine Cleaning — Not Replacing It

VitaCoat is engineered as a supplement to established cleaning and disinfection protocols. Routine cleaning removes soils and organic matter; VitaCoat provides a persistent antimicrobial layer that remains active between cleaning events; validation sampling can then be used to confirm surface performance in the deployed environment.

Routine cleaning

Scheduled cleaning removes soil and visible contamination.

VitaCoat barrier

Persistent layer remains active between cleaning events.

Compatibility

Standard approved cleaning protocols continue without redesign.

Validation

Pre- and post-application sampling can be used to confirm performance.
