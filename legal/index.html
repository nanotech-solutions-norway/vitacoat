Legal & Claim Boundaries

Use the Technical Material as the Source of Truth for Final Project Decisions

This page clarifies how public VitaCoat web content should be interpreted: as a structured overview for commercial and technical evaluation, not as a substitute for full technical documentation, project-specific review, or formal specification control.

Open DocumentationRequest Technical Review

Documentation dependence

Public web copy should be read alongside the full technical documentation and not in isolation.

Operating-condition qualifiers

Performance, durability, safety, and compatibility depend on substrate, cleaning chemistry, friction, environment, and use conditions.

Project-specific review

Compatibility and deployment decisions should be confirmed through representative review before procurement or scale rollout.

Examples of language to avoid

  • Universal-virus claims
  • Direct-food-contact assumptions without separate support
  • Claims that VitaCoat replaces cleaning
  • Unqualified durability claims in all environments

Preferred interpretation

  • Supplementary hygienic surface protection
  • Use-case and substrate qualification required
  • Documentation-backed performance framing
  • Procurement and technical review before deployment
This site is a decision-support and commercial-evaluation layer. Final specification, procurement, and deployment decisions should rely on the controlled VitaCoat documentation package and the relevant technical review process.
