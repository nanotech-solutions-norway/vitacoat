The Gamma draft highlights that surfaces can be clean immediately after disinfection, but quickly enter a new exposure window. VitaCoat is positioned as a continuous support layer within that interval.

Protection comparison

Short-term spray versus persistent coating

Conventional disinfection

Spray-and-wipe procedures are important measures, but their effect is largely concentrated around the period immediately after application. Once the active chemistry is spent, the surface returns to an unprotected state until the next cleaning event.

  • Protection window limited to the period after application
  • No persistent barrier after the window is over
  • Highly dependent on technique, contact time, and complete coverage at each cleaning event

VitaCoat persistent layer

VitaCoat bonds to treated surfaces and forms a durable antimicrobial matrix designed to remain active through repeated contact, routine cleaning, and the period between scheduled disinfection rounds.

  • Persistent function between cleaning intervals
  • Robust against repeated touch cycles
  • Can be combined with routine cleaning chemistry
Operational reality

The protection gap is larger than it appears

In many commercial and institutional environments, surfaces are formally disinfected one to three times per day. That still leaves long periods of unprotected contact during each operating phase. VitaCoat is designed to reduce this gap by maintaining a background layer of surface protection while standard cleaning continues normally.
