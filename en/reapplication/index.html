Reapplication

Planned renewal of the hygienic surface layer

Reapplication should ensure that VitaCoat function is maintained through the full service program. Timing should be set by actual wear, cleaning regime, substrate, operating load, and inspection - not by calendar date alone.

When renewal is considered

Four typical decision points

Planned service interval

Use the service window as the basis for preventive reapplication before clear performance decline.

Localized wear

High-load zones can be assessed separately for local renewal.

Special events

Heavy chemical exposure, damage, or repair may trigger a new assessment.

Program-managed operation

Integrate reapplication into facility or maintenance planning for predictable documentation.

Preparation

What should be reviewed before reapplication

Existing layer

Review visual finish, hydrophobic response, wear zones, and any damage.

Cleaning history

Review chemistry, frequency, and mechanical load since the previous treatment.

Surface preparation

Perform necessary cleaning, degreasing, and quality assurance before a new layer is applied.

Next step

Plan renewal before critical performance loss

For larger portfolios or multiple locations, reapplication should be logged with treatment date, area, operator, and next planned inspection.
