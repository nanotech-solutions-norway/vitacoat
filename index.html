How It Works

A Transparent Antimicrobial Barrier — Not a Momentary Disinfectant

VitaCoat is described in the source material as a thin, transparent, non-porous hard-surface coating built around two complementary components: natural citrus bioflavonoids as the active antimicrobial system, and a silicon dioxide (SiO₂) matrix that anchors those actives to the substrate.

The resulting surface is positioned as smoother, more hydrophobic, less prone to microbial adhesion, and easier to clean than an untreated hard surface.

Core Technology at a Glance

  • Active ingredient: citrus bioflavonoid complex
  • Matrix: silicon dioxide (SiO₂)
  • Appearance: transparent / invisible on application
  • Surface type: hard, non-porous substrates
  • Protection window: up to six months under normal use

Chemical Kill on Contact

The active system is presented as disrupting microbial cell structures at the surface interface.

Anti-Adhesion Surface Character

The SiO₂ matrix reduces attachment opportunities and supports easier removal of residues during routine cleaning.

Controlled Residual Action

The coating is positioned as a persistent, bonded layer that continues to function between cleaning events.

Traditional Spray-and-Wipe Disinfection

Creates a temporary window of surface cleanliness but leaves no continuity between one cleaning event and the next.

VitaCoat Persistent Protection Model

Creates a bonded surface layer designed to remain active between cleaning events, supplementing routine hygiene protocols over a multi-month cycle.

VitaCoat supplements routine cleaning and disinfection protocols. It is not positioned on this site as a replacement for those protocols.

Surface Compatibility

Hard, Non-Porous Surface Focus

Across the Gamma source and supporting technical materials, VitaCoat is consistently framed for hard, non-porous surfaces such as glass, metal, engineered plastics, laminates, and selected coated equipment surfaces.

High-abrasion and direct-food-contact contexts should not be inferred beyond the technical boundaries documented in the source material.

Compatibility Guardrails

  • Hard, non-porous surfaces only
  • Compatibility test recommended before scale deployment
  • High abrasion reduces coating service life
  • Not a flooring-first positioning
  • Food-area relevance does not equal direct-food-contact approval
  • Supplementary hygiene layer — not a cleaning replacement
Application Process

Four Steps to Six Months of Protection

1. Prepare Surface

Clean and degrease thoroughly. Remove incompatible residues, oils, or previous contaminants.

2. Apply & Polish Evenly

Apply a thin, even layer on the clean surface using the approved wipe-on application method.

3. Allow Curing Period

Respect the cure period before returning the surface to full service.

4. Maintain & Reapply

Continue normal gentle cleaning with reapplication aligned to the service interval.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is VitaCoat?

VitaCoat is presented as a transparent antimicrobial surface coating for hard, non-porous, high-touch surfaces.

How long does VitaCoat last?

The current VitaCoat site and source pack are built around an up-to-six-month service interval under normal-use conditions.

Does VitaCoat replace regular cleaning?

No. VitaCoat supplements cleaning and disinfection protocols; it is not positioned as a replacement for them.

Which surfaces is it suited for?

Hard, non-porous surfaces such as glass, metals, engineering plastics, laminates, and selected device or equipment housings.

Can VitaCoat be used in electronics and touchpoint environments?

Yes — that is one of the clearest application contexts in the source material, provided site-specific compatibility review is carried out before broad deployment.

What documentation is available?

Technical reports, standards summaries, compliance references, application guidance, maintenance guidance, and controlled document packs are all part of the VitaCoat documentation model.
