Persistent Protection Between Cleanings

Protection Comparison

Momentary Spray vs. Persistent Coating

Conventional disinfection

Sprays and wipes are essential, but their efficacy is largely exhausted within minutes to hours of application. Once the active chemistry dissipates, the surface returns to an unprotected baseline until the next cleaning round.

  • Protection window typically limited to the immediate application period
  • No residual barrier once the window closes
  • Highly dependent on operator technique, dwell time, and full coverage each cycle

VitaCoat persistent coating

VitaCoat bonds to treated surfaces at the molecular level, creating a durable antimicrobial matrix designed to remain active through repeated contact, routine cleaning, and the interval between scheduled disinfection events.

  • Persistent activity between cleaning events
  • Resilient through repeated touch cycles
  • Compatible with routine cleaning chemistry
Operational Reality

The Protection Gap Is Larger Than It Looks

In many commercial and institutional environments, surfaces are formally disinfected one to three times per day. That still leaves long periods of unprotected contact during every operational cycle. VitaCoat is designed to close that gap by maintaining a background layer of surface protection while standard cleaning continues unchanged.

Cleaning event

Surface is reset by routine cleaning or disinfection.

Protection decay

Momentary chemistry diminishes rapidly after the cleaning event.

Unprotected window

Surface is touched repeatedly with no residual barrier in place.

VitaCoat active

Persistent layer remains in place throughout the interval.

VitaCoat supplements existing cleaning and disinfection protocols. It closes the gap between cleaning cycles; it does not redefine or replace the hygiene program.
