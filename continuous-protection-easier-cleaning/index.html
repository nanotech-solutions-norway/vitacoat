Protection Comparison

Momentary Spray vs. Persistent Coating

Conventional disinfection

Sprays and wipes are essential, but their efficacy is largely exhausted within minutes to hours of application. Once the active chemistry dissipates, the surface returns to an unprotected baseline until the next cleaning round.

  • Protection window typically limited to the immediate application period
  • No residual barrier once the window closes
  • Highly dependent on operator technique, dwell time, and full coverage each cycle

VitaCoat persistent coating

VitaCoat bonds to treated surfaces at the molecular level, creating a durable antimicrobial matrix designed to remain active through repeated contact, routine cleaning, and the interval between scheduled disinfection events.

  • Persistent activity between cleaning events
  • Resilient through repeated touch cycles
  • Compatible with routine cleaning chemistry
Operational Reality

The Protection Gap Is Larger Than It Looks

In many commercial and institutional environments, surfaces are formally disinfected one to three times per day. That still leaves long periods of unprotected contact during every operational cycle. VitaCoat is designed to close that gap by maintaining a background layer of surface protection while standard cleaning continues unchanged.

Surface Science

Anti-Adhesion & Hydrophobic Surface Behavior

VitaCoat creates a densely cross-linked, low-surface-energy film that changes how liquids, biological matter, and particulates interact with the substrate. Instead of spreading and anchoring strongly to the surface, contaminants encounter a physical and chemical environment that discourages adhesion from the first moment of contact.

The hydrophobic character of the treated surface causes aqueous solutions, biological fluids, and cleaning residues to bead and roll off rather than wet and spread. At the same time, the molecular architecture reduces topographical anchor points for microbial colonization and proteinaceous debris.

Key surface parameters

  • High-hydrophobic surface behavior
  • Reduced surface energy vs. untreated substrates
  • Fewer effective adhesion anchor points
  • Lower dwell area for soils and moisture
Operational Impact

What Easier Cleaning Means in Practice

Reduced cleaning time

Soils release more readily, reducing total wipe passes and contact time per cleaning cycle.

Lower chemical burden

Lower-concentration cleaning agents can achieve validated cleanliness standards on treated surfaces.

Minimized droplet persistence

Residual moisture drains and evaporates faster on hydrophobic surfaces, shortening drying cycles.

Simplified validation protocols

More consistent surface behavior supports narrower variance in residue and swab recovery data.

Surface Comparison

Untreated vs. VitaCoat-Treated Surface Behavior

Untreated surface

  • Liquids spread and wet the surface
  • Strong adhesive bonds form with soils
  • Biofilm anchor points remain abundant
  • High mechanical force and stronger chemistry are often needed
  • Residual moisture persists longer after cleaning

VitaCoat-treated surface

  • Liquids bead immediately with lower contact area
  • Soils remain more loosely bound and easier to remove
  • Early-stage biofilm adhesion is disrupted
  • Lower wipe effort can achieve validated cleanliness
  • Fast-draining beads shorten drying cycles
