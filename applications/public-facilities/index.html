The Challenge

Continuous Traffic. Continuous Contact Pressure.

Public facilities are among the most demanding environments for surface hygiene management. Handrails, ticketing interfaces, counters, kiosks, and lift interiors may be touched hundreds or thousands of times per day. The time between scheduled cleaning rounds therefore becomes a sustained vulnerability window in which shared surfaces accumulate biological load from successive users.

Increasing cleaning frequency can reduce this exposure, but it also scales labor, chemistry, and staffing burden. VitaCoat is positioned to work as a supplement to those protocols rather than a replacement.

Key pressure points

  • Rapid recontamination between cleaning cycles
  • Structural staffing and cost constraints
  • User expectation and hygiene liability
  • High-volume shared infrastructure that cannot be constantly serviced
