Healthcare Applications

Persistent Hygienic Protection for Clinical High-Touch Surfaces

Healthcare environments are among the most demanding settings for surface hygiene management. Bed rails, door handles, nurse-station countertops, and shared equipment housings are contacted by dozens of individuals each shift. VitaCoat is positioned to bridge the recontamination window between scheduled cleaning events without changing existing infection-control protocols.

Critical Control Point

Why High-Touch Surfaces Matter in Clinical Settings

Standard cleaning and disinfection protocols remain the primary line of defense. However, routine disinfection also creates an unavoidable recontamination window: the interval between scheduled cleaning events during which surfaces can be recolonized. In a busy ward, that window can represent hours of unmanaged microbial accumulation across the surfaces patients and staff touch most frequently.

VitaCoat is designed to address that gap by providing a persistent supplemental barrier that remains active between disinfection events, not in place of them.

The recontamination window

  • 100+ contacts per shift on the most-used surfaces
  • 4–8 hour unmanaged interval between scheduled cleaning events
  • Persistent barrier remains active throughout the interval
  • Existing infection-control protocols remain unchanged

Technical Credibility

Standards Alignment & Surface Compatibility

Active chemistry

Natural citrus bioflavonoids anchored within a silicon dioxide (SiO₂) matrix designed to bond to hard, non-porous surfaces.

Duration of efficacy

Positioned for up to six months under normal-use conditions, supported by durability testing and application-interval logic.

Typical healthcare substrates

Powder-coated steel, anodized aluminum, stainless steel, treated glass, and compatible medical-grade engineering plastics.

Supplemental classification

VitaCoat is positioned as a supplemental hygienic surface treatment and not as a replacement for disinfectants, terminal cleaning, or infection-control protocols.

Maintenance Integration

Designed to Work With Existing Healthcare Cleaning Workflows

VitaCoat is designed to remain compatible with established environmental-services routines. Routine healthcare cleaning and disinfection using approved products should continue unchanged after application. Reapplication is recommended at the six-month interval, or earlier if a treated surface sustains significant physical damage that disrupts the bonded layer.

VitaCoat does not replace EPA-registered disinfectants, terminal cleaning, contact precautions, or any facility-specific infection-control requirement.
