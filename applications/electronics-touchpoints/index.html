Surface Risk Context

Why Shared Touchpoints Demand Persistent Protection

High-contact shared electronics are touched by dozens — often hundreds — of users each day, with minimal opportunity for meaningful cleaning between interactions. A surface cleaned at the start of a shift can be meaningfully recontaminated within minutes in a high-traffic environment.

This is not a failure of cleaning practice. It is the operational reality of public-facing touchpoints operating at scale, especially where optical clarity, device uptime, and user throughput limit how often aggressive cleaning can occur.

Why cleaning alone falls short

  • Continuous recontamination between interactions
  • Minimal opportunity for mid-cycle cleaning
  • Hard, non-porous device surfaces do not inhibit microbial survival on their own
  • Operational pressure to maintain uptime and user flow

Operational Fit

How VitaCoat Fits Into the Maintenance Program

VitaCoat is applied after a deep clean to a prepared surface and then left to function passively alongside the facility’s standard cleaning regimen. Its value is additive: cleaning addresses contamination that has already occurred, while the SiO₂-anchored bioflavonoid matrix remains active at the surface in the interval between those events.

Apply after deep clean

Coat a properly cleaned and prepared hard surface.

Continue routine cleaning

Maintain normal approved disinfection and hygiene practices.

Operate as normal

No special user handling or interface changes are required.

Reapply every 6 months

Refresh the coating in line with the standard service interval.

Material Compatibility

Transparent, Non-Corrosive, and Electrically Inert

Optically transparent

Positioned to preserve display brightness, colour accuracy, and touch sensitivity on suitable glass and display surfaces.

Non-corrosive

Designed without chlorine compounds, strong oxidisers, or abrasive particulates that would threaten common device housings.

Non-conductive

Relevant for capacitive touch interfaces and device exteriors where surface electrical properties must remain stable.

VitaCoat should be applied only to clean, dry, hard non-porous external surfaces. Compatibility should be verified on representative device samples before fleet-wide deployment. It is not intended for exposed circuitry, internal assemblies, or connector interfaces.
Technology

How the SiO₂ Bioflavonoid Matrix Works on Device Surfaces

SiO₂ anchor layer

A silicon dioxide network bonds mechanically to hard, non-porous substrates and supports durability against routine wiping and approved cleaning.

Natural bioflavonoid active

Citrus-derived bioflavonoids provide the hygienic barrier function within the matrix as a naturally derived, non-synthetic active system.

Rated duration

Up to six months under normal use conditions, subject to cleaning chemistry, frequency, and abrasion level.

Fleet-scale practicality

Application can be aligned with existing preventive-maintenance schedules without introducing a separate consumables-management burden.
