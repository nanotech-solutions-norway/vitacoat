Shared-Surface Challenge

Why Cleaning Alone Leaves a Predictable Gap

Desks, keyboards, door handles, shared screens, printers, and meeting-room touchpoints are contacted by rotating groups of students, staff, and visitors across the day. Even well-run cleaning schedules leave predictable intervals between interventions, especially during class changes, peak office hours, and turnover in shared spaces.

The challenge for facility teams is not whether gaps exist, but how to address them in a way that is cost-effective, low-disruption, and sustainable across entire buildings or campuses.

Operational pressures

  • High contact frequency on a small number of surfaces
  • Rotating populations and cross-contact risk
  • Large surface areas maintained by lean staffing
  • Need for low-disruption hygiene support during active hours

Formulation & Durability

How the SiO₂ Bioflavonoid Matrix Supports Institutional Use

Citrus bioflavonoid active layer

Naturally derived polyphenolic compounds provide the antimicrobial activity at the treated surface interface.

SiO₂ durable matrix

The silicon dioxide framework bonds to hard, non-porous substrates and supports the durability window under normal institutional use.

Transparent cured finish

No visible change to gloss, colour, or tactile quality under standard indoor conditions.

The validated service window is up to six months under normal institutional use. Duration remains surface- and use-intensity-dependent, so higher-touch surfaces will require earlier review than low-contact surfaces.
Low-Disruption Application

Designed to Fit Existing Facility Operations

Application is designed to integrate into standard facility rhythms without specialist equipment, extended downtime, or hazardous-material handling requirements. Once cured, routine cleaning can continue using standard products. Highly abrasive pads or aggressive bleach concentrations should be avoided if the full service interval is expected.

1. Surface preparation

Remove residues, oils, and particulate using standard facility cleaning before application.

2. Application

Apply with the recommended cloth or applicator in a uniform overlapping wipe pattern.

3. Cure

Allow the coating to air-cure before returning the surface to normal use.

4. Maintain

Continue routine cleaning and schedule reapplication according to surface category and use intensity.
